# Financing for PetStory.co — Funding Map in Portugal

> Focus: **non-repayable grants** (*fundo perdido*), with equity and debt complements when they fit for a health-tech/SaaS pet startup at MVP/seed stage.
> Updated April 2026.

Portuguese program names are kept verbatim because that's what you search for in the official portals. Surrounding analysis is in English.

---

## TL;DR — what fits PetStory.co today

The right framing depends on corporate setup and product stage. For an MVP pet-health app led by a senior founder in Lisbon, the realistic path combines three layers:

First, **non-dilutive startup cash** via *Vouchers para Startups — Novos Produtos Digitais/Tecnológicos* (IAPMEI / Startup Portugal), up to **€30,000 at 100% non-repayable grant**, plus *Vale Empreendedorismo / Vale Incubação* via an accredited incubator. Next, once there's traction, **SI Inovação Produtiva (Portugal 2030)** — 40–60% non-repayable, now in a hybrid model with a repayable financial instrument from Banco Português de Fomento. In parallel, **seed equity** with Portugal Ventures (Therapeutics & HealthTech arm), COREangels Lisbon, or Indico / Faber / Armilar. Later, with higher TRL and validated clinical component, an **EIC Accelerator** application (grant up to €2.5M + equity up to €10M).

The key caveat: almost none of these programs support "pure non-repayable" for B2C SaaS without strings. Portugal 2030 is migrating to a **mixed model** (grant + repayable instrument). 100% non-repayable exists only in small envelopes (vouchers) or in low-density territories — Lisbon does not qualify.

## 1. National Non-Repayable Support (Portugal 2030 + IAPMEI)

### 1.1 Vouchers para Startups — Novos Produtos Digitais/Tecnológicos

The most direct program for a digital startup at MVP stage. Run by IAPMEI via Startup Portugal, total envelope €30M in the 3rd edition, supporting up to **€30,000 per beneficiary at 100% non-repayable**. The 3rd edition dropped the mandatory green-component criterion, which significantly broadens eligibility. Typical application window is March–April; monitor IAPMEI for the next *aviso* (call). Eligible expenses cover HR, certifications, specialized services, equipment, and software — exactly the spend profile of an MVP.

### 1.2 StartUP Voucher (Innovate 2025–2026, IAPMEI)

Monthly stipend of **€900/month for 9 months** for pre-incorporation ideas, plus intermediate prizes (€1,500) and completion prizes (€2,000). Restricted to **applicants up to age 29**, degree holders without employment — therefore **does not apply to Thiago**, but worth citing if co-founders fit the profile. Applications closed 30 January 2026.

### 1.3 Vale Empreendedorismo

Supports companies **less than 2 years old**, no employee requirement, to contract specialized consulting (business plan, digital economy). Typical values in the €15–20k range at 75% non-repayable. Useful for validating the business case before applying for a larger *SI*.

### 1.4 Vale Incubação / Vales para Incubadoras e Aceleradoras (PT2030)

New Portugal 2030 measure, envelope **€20M**, support between **€30,000 and €150,000** via accredited incubators. The logical path: enter an incubator (Startup Lisboa, Beta-i, Fábrica de Startups, Building Global Innovators) and access this voucher through them.

### 1.5 SI Inovação Produtiva (Portugal 2030) — the big envelope

Portugal's main instrument for enterprise innovation financing. Rates:

- **40% non-repayable** across most of the territory (Lisbon is effectively residual — Lisbon has stricter rules as a "more developed" region).
- **Up to 60%** in low-density territories (Alto Alentejo, Beiras, Serra da Estrela).
- From May 2025, a **hybrid model** applies: up to 40% direct grant + up to 25% as a repayable financial instrument from Banco Português de Fomento, totaling ~50% support.

For PetStory this only kicks in when there's a substantial eligible investment (typically >€250k) — post-MVP, already preparing commercial launch. Important: Lisbon has a **much lower aid ceiling** than the rest of the country; worth validating whether to headquarter (at least operational parts) outside Lisbon if the project scales.

### 1.6 SI Inovação em Territórios de Baixa Densidade

If it makes business sense to locate operations outside major centers, **up to 50% fully non-repayable** aid is available exclusively for low-density regions. Applications open in two phases; the second closing **31 March 2026** (confirm current schedule on IAPMEI).

### 1.7 PRR — Agendas Mobilizadoras para a Inovação Empresarial

Not realistic for a solo startup: requires **consortia** with companies and SCTN entities, large average ticket size, and financial execution deadlines close on **31/12/2026**. Relevant only if PetStory joins as a minority partner in an existing consortium in animal / one-health. Worth monitoring reprogrammings via ANI and Compete2030.

## 2. Equity — VC, Business Angels, and Semi-Public Funds

Portugal's ecosystem raised ~€780M in VC in 2025 (vs. €320M in 2020), with strong seed and Series A activity. Relevant names for health-tech / SaaS:

**Portugal Ventures** — arm of Banco Português de Fomento with a dedicated *Therapeutics & HealthTech* department and 25+ health investments. Typical seed tickets, regular thematic calls. Most natural channel for a first public/private ticket in a pet health-tech.

**Indico Capital Partners** — seed to Series A in tech, tickets between €100k and €5M. Strong in software and digital health.

**Faber Ventures** — early-stage in Southern Europe, deep tech, climate, and digital. Portfolio includes SWORD Health, Unbabel, Codacy.

**Armilar Venture Partners** — ~€300M under management, focus on deep tech and hard science in healthcare and sustainability. Larger tickets, better suited to pre-Series A.

**COREangels Lisbon** — structured business-angel group, small early-stage tickets. Useful for the first pre-seed round.

**200M Fund** — €200M public fund managed by Banco Português de Fomento to **co-invest pari passu** with international VCs in startups scaling from Portugal. Does not invest alone — only comes in if there's already a private lead. Relevant for the seed / Series A round.

> **Pet-tech note:** no Portugal-based VC focuses specifically on pet tech. On the European map, Sifted tracks investors active in the space — Felix Capital, Talis Capital, and others. For PetStory, the path is to position as *digital health* (the pet niche is a differentiator, not a limiting category).

## 3. European Funds Accessible from Portugal

### 3.1 EIC Accelerator (European Innovation Council) — the "holy grail" European grant

2026 budget €634M (€414M open + €220M challenges). Offers **grant up to €2.5M + equity investment between €0.5M and €10M** ("blended finance"). From 2026 the process is simpler (20-page application forms instead of 50), faster (evaluations every 2 months), and features deeper technical review.

For PetStory, applying makes sense with a validated MVP, market evidence, and a differentiating tech component (AI, proprietary predictive model, veterinary medical device, etc.). Health tech is a supported area — clinical studies are typically TRL-6. Portugal's National Contact Point (NCP) for Horizon Europe, via ANI, provides free application-prep support.

### 3.2 EIC STEP Scale Up

**€10M to €30M** in investment (not pure grant) for scale-ups with European strategic technology. Budget €300M in 2025, projected €900M by 2027. Very specific focus on digital / deep tech, cleantech, biotech. Wrong stage for PetStory today — this is for later, post-Series A.

### 3.3 EIC Pre-Accelerator

More recent program, for earlier stages than the EIC Accelerator. Worth tracking via eic.ec.europa.eu as work programmes are published.

### 3.4 EIT Health

European health-innovation community with annual calls, bootcamps, and acceleration programs. Doesn't give large grants but provides access to clinical networks and validation — useful for a health-tech that needs veterinary / academic partners.

## 4. Other Complementary Support

**IFRRU / Banco Português de Fomento — subsidized credit lines.** Not non-repayable, but with rates well below market; can serve as private counterpart to SI Inovação Produtiva.

**Advance payment 40% → 25%.** Important to know: the non-repayable-incentive advance-payment mechanism was 40% until **31/12/2025**, and dropped to **25%** from 1/1/2026. This affects cash-flow for any Portugal 2030 application submitted now.

**SIFIDE II — Sistema de Incentivos Fiscais à I&D Empresarial.** Not cash; a tax credit (up to ~82% of eligible R&D spend), deductible against IRC (corporate tax). Only starts paying out when the company shows profit, but accumulates and carries forward for 8 years. For an AI-intensive health-tech, one of the most underused levers.

**StartUp Visa / Tech Visa.** Not financing, but opens the door to bringing non-EU talent without friction, effectively reducing hiring costs significantly.

## 5. Recommended sequencing for PetStory.co

Order matters as much as the programs themselves. A sequence that minimizes early dilution and leverages the non-cumulative nature of some envelopes:

Start with the **Voucher para Startups — Novos Produtos Digitais** (€30k non-repayable) to fund the MVP and first paying users. In parallel, join an **accredited incubator** and apply for the **Vale Incubação / Vales para Aceleradoras** for specialized services. With 6–9 months of traction, open conversations with **Portugal Ventures (Therapeutics & HealthTech) + COREangels** for a pre-seed round of €300–500k. With that runway and a validated product, apply to **SI Inovação Produtiva** (for scaling investment) and the **EIC Accelerator** (if the AI / clinical differentiator is defensible). **SIFIDE** runs in parallel from day one, accumulating tax credit against all R&D spend.

Risk to avoid: **don't apply the same project to multiple programs with overlapping expenses.** Portuguese systems cross-check applications and disqualify for duplication.

---

## 6. Practical next steps

Concrete moves to make: (a) confirm the next *Vouchers para Startups* call on the IAPMEI portal (typically opens Mar–Apr); (b) contact 2–3 Lisbon-accredited incubators and check which have Vale Incubação "shelf"; (c) schedule a pre-screening conversation with Portugal Ventures Therapeutics & HealthTech to validate whether the pet angle qualifies for their mandate; (d) subscribe to alerts from Compete2030, ANI, and Balcão dos Fundos so you don't miss application windows; (e) start documenting R&D expenses from day one for future SIFIDE.
